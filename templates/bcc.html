BASAL CELL CARCINOMA DETECTED

Basal cell carcinoma (BCC) is the most common form of skin cancer. It arises from the basal cells, which are found in the deepest layer of the epidermis. BCC usually appears as a slow-growing, raised, and pearly bump on the skin, often with visible blood vessels or a central depression.

Basic Treatement

Basic treatments for basal cell carcinoma include:

  • Surgical excision: Surgically removing the tumor along with a margin of healthy tissue to ensure complete removal.
  • Mohs surgery: A specialized surgical technique that removes the tumor layer by layer and is particularly effective for large or recurring BCCs.
  • Electrodessication and curettage (ED&C): Scraping off the tumor and then using an electric current to destroy any remaining cancer cells.
  • Cryotherapy: Freezing the tumor with liquid nitrogen to remove it.
  • Topical medications: For superficial BCCs, prescription creams like imiquimod or 5-fluorouracil can be applied.

Diagnostic

Diagnosis of basal cell carcinoma is typically done through a skin biopsy, where a small sample of the affected area is taken and examined under a microscope to confirm the presence of cancerous cells.

Early detection and treatment are crucial in managing basal cell carcinoma effectively. Regular skin examinations by a dermatologist and adopting sun protection measures are essential in preventing its development and identifying it at an early stage.If you notice any suspicious changes in your skin, consult a dermatologist or healthcare professional immediately.
